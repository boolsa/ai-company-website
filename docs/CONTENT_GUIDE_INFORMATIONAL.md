# Microve.ai Content Guide — Informational Site

## Purpose of This Site

This is an **informational and philosophical website**, not a sales funnel. Its purpose is to:
- Clearly explain what we do and how we do it
- Articulate our philosophy and approach
- Educate visitors about micro-automation
- Build trust through transparency
- Help visitors self-select (decide if we're right for them)

**What this site does NOT do:**
- Push for conversions
- Use sales tactics or urgency
- Make promises we can't keep
- Hide information to force contact

---

## Core Principles for All Content

### 1. Plain Language First
**Before:** "We map AI capabilities to your workflow bottlenecks"  
**After:** "We look at what's wasting your time, then pick the right technology to automate it"

**Before:** "Operational friction affects your bottom line"  
**After:** "Small inefficiencies add up when you multiply them across your whole team"

### 2. Show What Things Actually Are
**Problem:** The site constantly mentions "AI tools" but never shows what they look like  
**Solution:** Every abstract concept needs a concrete example or visual

### 3. Define Your Terms
**Problem:** Words like "micro-automation," "operator," "service engagement" are used without definition  
**Solution:** Define terms the first time you use them, or use plain language instead

### 4. Be Specific About What You Won't Do
**Philosophy:** Saying no builds credibility  
**Implementation:** List specific scenarios where you refuse work

## Page-by-Page Content Guidelines

### Homepage

**Purpose:** Explain what we do in 30 seconds

**Required Elements:**
1. One-sentence description of micro-automation (with example)
2. What you actually get (deliverables)
3. Our philosophy in one paragraph
4. Link to "How We Work" for details
5. Simple navigation to other pages

**Tone:** Helpful, straightforward, no hype

**NO:** CTAs, "Book Now" buttons, urgency language

---

### What We Do Page

**Purpose:** Detailed explanation of our approach

**Required Sections:**
1. **What Micro-Automation Means** — Define with concrete examples
2. **What We Actually Build** — Show what deliverables look like
3. **How We Work** — The 4-step process (Listen → Question → Propose → Build)
4. **What We Won't Do** — List of refusals (builds credibility)
5. **Who We Help** — Types of businesses/situations we're right for

**Tone:** Educational, transparent, honest about limitations

---

### Philosophy Page (New)

**Purpose:** Articulate the "why" behind our approach

**Required Sections:**
1. **Why Micro?** — Why small solutions beat big transformations
2. **Why Custom?** — Why we don't sell products
3. **Why Listen First?** — The importance of questioning the brief
4. **Why AI + Rules?** — Technologyagnostic approach
5. **Our Anti-Hype Stance** — What we think is wrong with AI marketing

**Tone:** Opinionated, thoughtful, educational

---

### Cases Page

**Purpose:** Show real examples of what we've built

**Required Elements for Each Case:**
1. What the client originally asked for
2. What we discovered was the real problem
3. What we built (with screenshot/mockup if possible)
4. The actual result (hours saved, errors reduced)
5. What it cost (transparency builds trust)

**NEW Required Section:** "What They Asked For vs. What We Built" — Shows we question briefs

**Tone:** Factual, specific, honest about the process

---

### About Page

**Purpose:** Establish credibility through experience

**Required Sections:**
1. **Who We Are** — Names, photos, specific business backgrounds
2. **Why We Started This** — Origin story with specifics
3. **What We've Built** — Previous businesses/companies with metrics
4. **Our Approach** — How business experience changes our work

**Tone:** Personal, experienced, humble about what we know and don't know

---

### Technology Page (New)

**Purpose:** Demystify what we use

**Required Sections:**
1. **AI Tools We Use** — OpenAI, Claude, etc. (be specific)
2. **Automation Platforms** — Make, Zapier, etc.
3. **How We Choose** — Criteria for picking one over another
4. **When We Don't Use AI** — Honest about limitations

**Tone:** Transparent, educational, no mystique

---

### Pricing Page

**Purpose:** Give visitors budget context

**Required Elements:**
1. **Price Ranges** — At minimum: "Most projects $X-$Y"
2. **What Drives Cost** — Complexity, integrations, custom AI
3. **What's Included** — Build, documentation, training, support
4. **What's Not Included** — Third-party software fees

**Tone:** Transparent, helpful for planning

**NO:** "Contact us for quote" as the only option

---

### Contact Page

**Purpose:** Allow interested visitors to reach out

**Required Elements:**
1. Simple contact form (Name, Email, Message)
2. Email address
3. Response time expectation (e.g., "We reply within 24 hours")
4. Brief note about what happens when you contact us

**NO:** High-pressure language, required phone number, "Book a call" as only option

## Content Checklist

Every page must:
- [ ] Define terms the first time they're used
- [ ] Include at least one concrete example
- [ ] Answer "so what?" for every claim
- [ ] Use plain language (readable by a high schooler)
- [ ] Include specific numbers where possible
- [ ] Acknowledge limitations honestly

Every page must NOT:
- [ ] Use jargon without definition
- [ ] Make vague promises
- [ ] Hide information to force contact
- [ ] Use sales pressure tactics
- [ ] Assume visitor knowledge

---

## Tone Guidelines

**DO:**
- Write like you're explaining to a smart friend
- Use specific examples
- Admit what you don't know or won't do
- Show your work
- Be humble about AI's current capabilities

**DON'T:**
- Write like a consultant
- Use buzzwords to sound smart
- Promise outcomes you can't guarantee
- Hide behind abstractions
- Hype AI as magic
