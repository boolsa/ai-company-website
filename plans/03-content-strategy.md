# Content Strategy & Writing Guidelines

## Core Principle: Clarity Over Cleverness

Every sentence must answer: "Would a smart business owner who's never heard of us understand this?"

---

## Words to Replace

| Instead of | Use | Why |
|------------|-----|-----|
| Micro + Improvement | Custom A.I. Automation | Current tagline is cute but meaningless |
| Most A.I. is an encyclopedia | ChatGPT answers questions. Our tools take action. | Cut the metaphor, use specifics |
| Map A.I. capabilities | Match specific A.I. functions | "Map capabilities" is consultant speak |
| Purpose built tools | Single-purpose automations | "Purpose built" is jargon |
| Service engagement | You hire us to build and run your tools | Clear relationship |
| The gap between knowing and doing | Stop asking A.I. questions. Start letting A.I. handle tasks. | Philosophy → action |
| Operators | Your team / people who do the work | Undefined jargon |
| Capabilities | Functions or features | Vague → specific |
| Implementation | Setup and launch | Plain English |
| Workflow | How work moves through your team | Define by example |

---

## Structure Guidelines

### Hero Section Must Include:
1. Clear headline stating what you do (not metaphorical)
2. One concrete example of a tool in action
3. Integration logos (show where tools live)
4. Brief explanation of how it's different

### Why Microve Exists Section Must Include:
1. Specific contrast (ChatGPT vs. your tools)
2. Before/After example showing the difference
3. Clear service model explanation

### Process Section (4 Steps) Must Include:
1. **Listen:** Example problems prospects might have
2. **Propose:** Specific A.I. functions mentioned
3. **Build:** Where the tool lives (Slack, Salesforce, etc.)
4. **Launch:** Specific metrics tracked

---

## Where to Add Examples

**Hero Section:** One concrete tool example (logistics company, Monday.com, email parsing)

**Why Microve Exists:** Before/After scenario (10 hours sorting tickets → A.I. sorts automatically)

**Step 1 (Listen):** Example problems: "10 hours a week sorting support tickets"

**Step 2 (Propose):** List functions: "document reading, data extraction, classification"

**Step 3 (Build):** Specify location: "Slack, Salesforce, your email, wherever you work"

**Step 4 (Launch):** Name metrics: "hours saved, errors reduced, tasks completed"

---

## The Assumption Test

For every sentence, ask:
1. Would my mom understand this?
2. Can they picture what this looks like?
3. Do they know what the deliverable is?
4. Is there a concrete example?

If the answer to any is "no," rewrite it.

---

## What We Don't Do (For Clarity)

**We don't use:**
- Metaphors (encyclopedia, operators, etc.)
- Consultant speak (map capabilities, engagement, etc.)
- Vague promises (measurable results, purpose built, etc.)
- Abstract philosophy without examples

**We do use:**
- Concrete examples
- Specific technologies
- Plain English
- Before/After scenarios

## Homepage Structure (Preserved)

1. **Hero**
   - Tagline: Custom A.I. Automation
   - Headline: We Build Custom A.I. Tools That Automate Your Business Tasks
   - Subhead: Single-purpose automations—each one handles one specific task
   - Example: Logistics company, Monday.com, email parsing
   - Integration logos

2. **Why Microve Exists**
   - Headline: ChatGPT Answers Questions. Our Tools Take Action.
   - Paragraph: Specific contrast with examples
   - Before/After box: Concrete scenario
   - Service model explanation

3. **The Process (4 Steps)**
   - Listen (with example problems)
   - Propose (with specific functions)
   - Build (with where it lives)
   - Launch (with specific metrics)

4. **What You Get (New Section)**
   - Working tool
   - Documentation
   - Training
   - Ownership note

5. **Footer**
   - Contact info
   - Typical project timeline
   - Copyright
