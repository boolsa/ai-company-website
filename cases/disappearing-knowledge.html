1 We Listen

A mailroom operations manager in New York City with 4 positions and rotating new hires. They had SOPs and know-how documents, but everything was scattered—buried in physical file piles, lost on servers, or trapped in veteran employees' heads. New hires spent 10-15 minutes searching for answers to basic questions, often giving up and interrupting busy colleagues.

2 We Audit

We reviewed their training process and gathered all relevant documents. The problem wasn't missing information—it was unfindable information. Documents existed but were functionally useless because employees couldn't access them quickly. When people left, their knowledge walked out the door. The manager needed a way to compile everything in one place where it wouldn't get lost again.

3 We Design

Design a private RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) system that employees can access anytime. Two micro-automations: one to continuously ingest new documents and prevent them from getting lost again, another to enable instant knowledge retrieval through natural language chat so employees get answers in 30 seconds instead of 15 minutes.

4 We Implement

Continuous Document Ingestion

  • → Captures scattered physical & digital docs
  • → Auto-processes new SOPs as written
  • → Updates knowledge base when procedures change
  • → Everything stored in one permanent location

Instant Knowledge Retrieval

  • → Natural language chat interface
  • → Searches across all documents instantly
  • → Provides synthesized answers with sources
  • → Private access—employees only

The Result

Scattered piles became a living knowledge system. New hires train themselves. Veterans' tacit knowledge is captured before they leave. Documents finally serve their purpose.
