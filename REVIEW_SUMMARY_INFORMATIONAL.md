## KEY CHANGES FOR INFORMATIONAL SITE

### 1. NO CTAs Except Contact
**Removed:**
- "Book a call" buttons
- "Get started" CTAs
- "See pricing" as primary action
- Urgency language
- Trust bars with conversion focus

**Kept:**
- Simple "Contact" link in nav
- Contact form on Contact page
- Informational links ("Learn more about X")

### 2. Plain Language Replacements

| Jargon | Plain Language |
|--------|----------------|
| Operator | People who've run businesses |
| Micro-automation | Small tools that handle one workflow |
| Service engagement | You hire us, we build it, you own it |
| Map AI capabilities | Pick the right technology |
| Bottleneck | Where work gets stuck |
| Operational friction | Frustrating processes |
| Workflow | How work moves through your team |

### 3. Philosophy Made Explicit

**NEW: "Why Micro?" Section**
Big AI projects fail because they try to boil the ocean. We solve one problem completely before moving to the next. Small wins compound. Big bangs blow up.

**NEW: "What We Won't Do" Section**
We say no to about 80% of requests. Lists specific refusals:
- Chatbots for basic FAQs
- "Digital transformation" projects
- Projects over a month
- Solutions requiring workflow changes

### 4. Concrete Examples Everywhere

**OLD:** "We build AI tools for workflow optimization"
**NEW:** "Software watches the email inbox, reads each receipt image using AI, extracts the relevant data, and enters it directly into QuickBooks"

**Examples now include:**
- The actual problem (4 hours/day of manual data entry)
- The actual solution (AI reads receipts, enters into QuickBooks)
- The actual result (4 hours → 15 minutes)

### 5. Transparency on Technology

**NEW: "Technology We Use" Section**
Lists specific tools:
- OpenAI (GPT-4), Anthropic (Claude), Google (Gemini)
- Make.com, Zapier, n8n
- Python, Node.js

Explains: "We choose based on your problem, not our preferences. Sometimes the best solution is a simple spreadsheet formula."

### 6. Clear Pricing Ranges

**OLD:** "Pricing follows workflow complexity"
**NEW:** "Most micro-automations: $2,400 – $8,000"

Breaks down:
- Simple: $2,400–$4,000
- Complex: $5,000–$8,000
- Custom with dashboards: higher

### 7. "What You Actually Get" Section

Defines deliverables clearly:
1. A Working Tool (runs in your existing software)
2. Documentation (how it works, how to fix it)
3. 30-Minute Training

Emphasizes: "You own everything. No monthly fees. No platform to log into."

## MOCK SITE STRUCTURE

The `mock-informational-site.html` is a complete single-page informational site:

### Sections (No CTAs):
1. **Header** — Simple explanation of what micro-automation means
2. **What You Get** — The 3 deliverables + ownership note
3. **What It Looks Like** — 3 concrete examples with before/after
4. **How We Work** — 4-step process (Listen → Question → Propose → Build)
5. **Philosophy** — Why micro, why custom, anti-hype stance
6. **What We Won't Do** — List of refusals (builds credibility)
7. **Selected Cases** — 2 examples with "asked for vs. actually needed"
8. **About** — Who we are, why we started this
9. **Technology** — Specific stack, how we choose
10. **Pricing** — Transparent ranges, what's included
11. **Contact** — Simple form, email, response time

**Navigation:** What We Do | Philosophy | Cases | About | Pricing | Contact

**NO:** Book Now buttons, Get Started CTAs, sticky action bars, popups, chat widgets

## CRITICAL ASSUMPTIONS ADDRESSED

### 1. "Micro-automation" is undefined
**FIX:** Homepage now defines it immediately: "A micro-automation handles ONE specific workflow bottleneck—like processing expense receipts or qualifying sales leads."

### 2. "AI tools" is abstract
**FIX:** Three detailed examples showing exactly what the tools do, with specific technologies and workflows.

### 3. "Operator" is jargon
**FIX:** Replaced with "people who've run businesses" and specific credentials: "Former COO of X. Built Y to Z revenue."

### 4. "Integration" is vague
**FIX:** Examples show exactly how data flows: "Email → AI reads → QuickBooks updated"

### 5. Process is repeated across pages
**FIX:** Consolidated to ONE clear 4-step process on the main page.

### 6. No proof of "questioning briefs"
**FIX:** Each case study includes "What they asked for vs. what they actually needed"

### 7. Philosophy is buried
**FIX:** Dedicated Philosophy section with "Why Micro?" "Why Custom?" "Anti-Hype"

### 8. Team is faceless
**FIX:** About section includes photos (placeholders), names, specific business backgrounds

## CONTENT PRINCIPLES (Informational Site)

### Every Page Must:
1. Define terms the first time they're used
2. Include at least one concrete example
3. Answer "so what?" for every claim
4. Use plain language (readable by a high schooler)
5. Include specific numbers where possible
6. Acknowledge limitations honestly

### Every Page Must NOT:
1. Use jargon without definition
2. Make vague promises
3. Hide information to force contact
4. Use sales pressure tactics
5. Assume visitor knowledge
