We Listen

A logistics company with 16 field managers across the country. The accounting person spent 12 hours/week manually processing expense reports—creating folders, downloading receipt images, transcribing data, and filing everything. The owner couldn't get managers to use expense software, so email with photo attachments was the only workflow that worked.

2

3

We Design

Design a system that keeps the manager workflow exactly the same (email photos) but automates everything on the back end. Two micro-automations working together: one to extract data from receipt images using AI vision, another to automatically organize files and folders without human touch.

The KPI Detective Work

Store owner deeply cared about fairly rewarding employees, but tracking individual performance across 4 rotating locations was nearly impossible using fragmented POS and payroll data.

~10
hours/week saved
from being wasted
1

We Listen

A dog supply store owner with 4 Las Vegas locations believed deeply in fairly rewarding his team. But with 3-4 employees rotating across stores simultaneously, figuring out who drove sales was a nightmare. The only hard data came when an employee logged into the POS register—ignoring the floor staff who actually helped the customer decide. He spent 8-12 hours weekly cross-referencing scheduling data with sales spikes to guess who was performing, but the results were opaque and delayed by weeks.

2

We Audit

We mapped the data silos: Homebase (scheduling) and Square (POS). The owner was making educated guesses, meaning high performers were being overlooked simply because they weren't the ones ringing up the transaction. The transparency he valued was impossible without finding hidden correlations between foot traffic, shift overlaps, and category-specific sales spikes—a puzzle no human could solve manually in a reasonable timeframe.

3

We Design

Design a system that continuously ingests data from both platforms and uses statistical modeling to attribute sales influence even when staff aren't logged at the register. Two micro-automations: an ETL pipeline that unifies scheduling and transaction data, and an AI analysis engine that weights store busyness, shift compositions, and product categories to score true performance fairly.

4

We Implement

Cross-Platform Data Integration
  • → API sync with Square POS (transactions, timestamps)
  • → API sync with Homebase (actual shift punch-ins)
  • → Normalizes employee IDs across disparate systems
  • → Maps transaction velocity to exact shift overlaps
AI Performance Pattern Analysis
  • → Analyzes sales uplift during specific staff combos
  • → Weights performance against baseline location traffic
  • → Sends Friday SMS alerts for top weekly performers
  • → Generates transparent, fair leaderboard dashboard

Result: Improved Business

The owner now relies on fair, defensible performance insights that account for the complexity of rotating staff. Employees trust the system because it acknowledges floor work, not just register log-ins. Best of all, rewards are given in real-time, keeping morale consistently high across all four stores.

The Weekly Data Scrape

An insurance broker lost hours every week manually logging into multiple carrier portals just to download data and paste it into his custom Excel dashboard.

~6
hours/week saved
from being wasted
1

We Listen

An independent insurance broker had a highly specific, time-consuming weekly routine. To keep his business running, he needed data from multiple different insurance carrier portals. Every week, he would manually log into each website, navigate through clumsy menus, download the raw data files, and manually input the numbers into a custom Excel dashboard he had built. It was a mindless, frustrating chore that took up a large chunk of his valuable selling time.

2

We Audit

We analyzed his workflow and initially proposed three solutions: 1) A browser agent to auto-login and scrape the data, 2) A script to format and inject that data into his existing Excel sheet, and 3) Building a brand-new, robust web dashboard to replace Excel entirely. The client reviewed the options and made a smart operational decision: he loved his Excel dashboard and didn't want the chore of learning a new tool. He just wanted the data collection automated.

3

We Design

Design a background system that respects the client's current workflow without forcing new software adoption. Two micro-automations: one AI browser agent capable of navigating authenticated carrier portals to scrape the required files, and a data-processing script that automatically structures the raw data and inputs it directly into his existing beloved Excel dashboard.

4

We Implement

Authenticated Browser Agent
  • → Logs into multiple insurance carrier portals
  • → Navigates complex, dynamic web menus
  • → Identifies and downloads weekly data reports
  • → Runs reliably in the background every weekend
Excel Data Pipeline
  • → Extracts numbers from downloaded CSV/PDFs
  • → Formats data to match the broker's specific layout
  • → Injects the data directly into his existing Excel sheet
  • → Preserves all of his custom formulas and charts

Result: Improved Business
