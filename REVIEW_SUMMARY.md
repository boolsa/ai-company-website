## THE BRUTAL VERDICT

> "The content assumes readers can translate abstract A.I. concepts into their actual business problems without ever showing what a 'tool' actually looks like or does."

**Core Problem:** The site uses metaphors, jargon, and abstractions instead of concrete examples. Readers can't visualize what they're buying.

✅ Same navigation structure

✅ Same integration logos section

## WHAT WAS CHANGED (Content)

### Hero Section

**Tagline:**
- Before: "Micro + Improvement"
- After: "CUSTOM A.I. AUTOMATION"
- **Why:** Cute → Clear

**Headline:**
- Before: "We Build Practical A.I. Tools for Your Business"
- After: "We Build Custom A.I. Tools That Automate Your Business Tasks"
- **Why:** "Practical" is weak. "Automate" is the value. "Tasks" makes it concrete.

**Subhead:**
- Before: "Focused, purpose built A.I. tools..."
- After: "Single-purpose automations—each one handles one specific task in your workflow..."
- **Why:** "Purpose built" is jargon. "Single-purpose automations" is clear.

**Added:**
- Concrete example: "We built a tool for a logistics company that reads incoming shipment emails..."
- **Why:** Without an example, this is all abstract

---

### Why Microve Exists Section

**Headline:**
- Before: "Most A.I. is an encyclopedia. Your business needs tools that do actual work."
- After: "ChatGPT Answers Questions. Our Tools Take Action."
- **Why:** Cut the metaphor. Use specific contrast.

**Added:**
- Before/After box: "Before: Your team spends 10 hours/week... After: A.I. reads, categorizes..."
- **Why:** Shows practical difference, not philosophical

**Paragraph:**
- Before: "We provide a service..."
- After: "You don't buy our software. You hire us to build, run, and maintain your automation..."
- **Why:** Clear service model, not vague "engagement"

---

### Process Section (4 Steps)

**Step 1: Listen**
- Added: "Example problems: 'We spend 10 hours a week sorting support tickets'..."
- **Why:** Makes abstract concrete

**Step 2: Propose**
- Changed: "Map A.I. capabilities" → "Identify which A.I. functions can solve your problem—document reading, data extraction, classification..."
- **Why:** Lists actual functions so prospects understand what A.I. can do

**Step 3: Design → Build**
- Changed: "Design and build A.I. tools" → "Build the automation and integrate it into your existing software—Slack, Salesforce, your email, wherever you work"
- **Why:** Tells them WHERE the tool lives

**Step 4: Implement → Launch**
- Changed: "Measure what they do" → "Track hard numbers: hours saved, errors reduced, tasks completed. You see exactly what it's doing."
- **Why:** Names the metrics. Adds transparency.

---

### Added Section: What You Get

**New section clarifies deliverables:**
1. A Working Tool
2. Documentation
3. Training

**Ownership box:** "You own everything. No monthly fees to us. No platform to log into."
- **Why:** The #1 confusion is "what am I actually buying?"

---

### Footer

**Added:**
- Contact email: hello@microve.ai
- Typical project timeline: "2-4 week build"
- **Why:** No contact info on homepage was a gap
